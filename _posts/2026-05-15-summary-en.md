This vulnerability affects Nginx, one of the most widely used web servers globally, and could allow unauthenticated attackers to achieve remote code execution or denial of service, posing a significant security risk to millions of websites. The exploit requires a rewrite directive with a question mark in the replacement string, followed by a set directive referencing an unnamed PCRE capture group (e.g., $1). The published proof-of-concept assumes ASLR is disabled, though the disclosure claims a reliable ASLR bypass exists.

**Background**: Nginx is a high-performance web server and reverse proxy used by over 30% of websites. The rewrite module processes URL rewriting rules, and improper handling of unnamed PCRE captures combined with a question mark in the replacement string can cause a heap buffer overflow. ASLR (Address Space Layout Randomization) is a security technique that randomizes memory addresses to make exploitation harder.

## [Mean Shift Interacting Particle Systems for Bayesian Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14142) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A new paper introduces interacting particle system methods that minimize maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) to approximate Bayesian inference, extending classical mean shift to continuous distributions with both gradient-free and gradient-informed variants. These methods address key challenges in Bayesian computation, such as mode collapse and scaling to high dimensions, offering a principled and efficient alternative to traditional Markov chain Monte Carlo and variational inference. The dynamics for MMD minimization are invariant to the unknown normalizing constant, enabling direct use with unnormalized densities. The methods converge quickly, capture anisotropy and multi-modality, and avoid mode collapse.

rss · arXiv - Data Science & Statistics · May 15, 04:00

**Background**: Bayesian inference often requires computing expectations under a target distribution, which can be approximated by quadrature rules. Maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) is a kernel-based metric for comparing distributions, and interacting particle systems are stochastic processes where particles interact to approximate a target measure. The mean shift algorithm is a classic mode-seeking technique for density estimation.

## [Theoretical Limits of Fairness in Conformal Prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14260) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A new paper derives a conservation law and lower bound showing that pooled calibration in conformal prediction inevitably causes group-wise coverage distortion, and proves that Equalized Coverage and Equalized Set Size are fundamentally in tension. This work reveals fundamental trade-offs in fairness-oriented conformal prediction, providing a principled framework for practitioners to understand and navigate the inevitable distortion between coverage and set size fairness. The paper studies split conformal calibration and quantifies the cost of moving between group-wise and pooled calibration policies, with experiments on synthetic and real data confirming the bidirectional trade-off after finite-sample calibration.

rss · arXiv - Data Science & Statistics · May 15, 04:00

**Background**: Conformal prediction is a distribution-free method for uncertainty quantification that produces prediction sets with guaranteed coverage. Split conformal prediction uses a separate calibration set to determine a threshold for nonconformity scores. When applied across groups with different score distributions, a single pooled threshold can lead to unequal coverage or set sizes across groups, raising fairness concerns.

## [KRR Saturation & Multiple Descent Extended to Product Kernels](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14524) ⭐️ 8.0/10

This paper generalizes the saturation effect and multiple descent behavior from inner product kernels on the sphere to a broad family of product kernels in large-dimensional kernel ridge regression. This extension significantly broadens the theoretical understanding of kernel ridge regression, showing that key phenomena like saturation and multiple descent are not limited to restrictive kernel settings, which has implications for the design and analysis of kernel methods in high dimensions. The paper establishes minimax optimality when the source condition s ≤ 1, a saturation effect when s > 1, and a periodic plateau phenomenon in convergence rates leading to multiple-descent behavior with respect to sample size n.

rss · arXiv - Data Science & Statistics · May 15, 04:00

**Background**: Kernel ridge regression (KRR) is a nonparametric regression method that combines kernel methods with ridge regularization. The saturation effect refers to KRR failing to achieve the information-theoretic lower bound when the target function is overly smooth, while multiple descent describes the risk curve having multiple peaks and valleys as sample size increases. Previous theoretical results on these phenomena were largely confined to inner product kernels on the sphere or kernels with strong eigenfunction assumptions like hypercontractivity.
